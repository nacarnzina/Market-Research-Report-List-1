<p><ul><li>Diuretics</li><li>β-blockers</li><li>Calcium Channel Blockers</li><li>Renin-angiotensin System Inhibitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The oral antihypertensive drug market comprises various types, including diuretics, β-blockers, calcium channel blockers, and renin-angiotensin system inhibitors. Diuretics reduce blood volume by promoting sodium and water excretion. β-blockers decrease heart rate and cardiac output, lowering blood pressure. Calcium channel blockers relax blood vessels by inhibiting calcium entry into cells, enhancing blood flow. Renin-angiotensin system inhibitors, including ACE inhibitors and ARBs, disrupt the renin-angiotensin system to decrease blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health. Each class plays a vital role in managing hypertension.</p></p>
