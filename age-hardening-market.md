<p><p>Age hardening, a heat treatment process, enhances the strength of materials, especially aluminum alloys. In the artificial aging market, heat treatment is applied post-shaping to accelerate precipitation hardening, improving mechanical properties quickly. Conversely, the natural aging market relies on time and temperature, allowing the material to strengthen gradually. Both methods are crucial in industries like aerospace and automotive, where enhanced durability and performance are essential. The choice between artificial and natural aging depends on the specific application requirements and material properties.</p></p>
