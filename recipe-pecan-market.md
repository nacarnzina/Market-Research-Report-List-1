<p><p>The pecan market is primarily divided into two types: in-shell pecans and shelled pecans. In-shell pecans are sold with their hard outer shells intact, appealing to consumers seeking freshness and longer shelf life. They are often used in baking and snacking. Shelled pecans, on the other hand, have the outer shell removed, making them convenient for culinary uses and quicker preparation. Both market segments cater to different consumer preferences and applications in cooking, baking, and snacking.</p></p>
